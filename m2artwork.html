Art Work of Salar Jung Museum, Hyderabad

Throne of Tutankhamun

It is made of wood and covered with gold and silver. It is ornamented with semi-precious stones and colored glass. The throne meant, not only the link justify-content-between the worlds of Gods and the people, but also majesty, stability, safety and balance.

Ivory chair

The ivory chair was gifted to Tipu Sultan by King Louis XVI, 18th century, India.

Huqqa botton

Josiah Wedgwood and Sons

Wedgwood eearthern ware is generally decorated with a feieze of classical fifures in creamy white using a background of blue, green and lavender. This Huqqa base in blue and white depicting classical figures, trees and leaves was made especially for Salar Jung I when he visited England in 1876.

Antique Chinese ivory carving

The ivory carving ball has several smaller balls inside.

Parrot

The Parrot is made and worked in gold, embellished with precious and semi-precious stones and coloured enamel. The Parrot, inlaid with green, red and colourless stones with an engraved and chiselled gold framework, is perched on a tree holding a flower bud with its beak.

Jde Quran stand

Miniature stand in jade(rehal), dated 1209 - 1210 CE, inscribed with the name of Iltutmish, ruler of Delhi Sultan.
